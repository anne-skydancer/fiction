*She slipped out of bed, careful not to disturb Anne, and padded to the living room. The first light was just beginning to touch the hills, painting them in shades of rose and gold.*

*She set up her easel by the window. Retrieved her sketchbook and pencils.*

*And she drew.*

*Not Anne, this time. Not Julia. Not Dani or Margot or any of the people who had shaped her.*

*She drew herself.*

*A woman kneeling in morning light, collar gleaming at her throat, eyes turned toward a window where the sun was rising. A woman who had been lost and found. Who had been terrified and learned to fly. Who had been broken and remade into something stronger.*

*She drew until the sun cleared the hills, until the light shifted from rose to gold to clear morning white.*

*Then she set down her pencil, touched the collar at her throat, and smiled.*

*Somewhere in the house, she heard Anne stirring. Heard footsteps in the hallway. Heard the familiar rhythm of the day beginning.*

*She rose to make coffee.*

---

*Later that morning, Anne drove the Mercedes down Highway 29.*

*The spark plugs were new. The engine ran smooth. The road unwound before her like a promise.*

*She wasn't running from anything.*

*She was driving toward.*

*Toward the valley she loved. Toward the practice that gave her purpose. Toward the house where Amy would be waiting, collar gleaming, smile bright.*

*Toward life.*

*The sun was warm through the windshield. The vineyards stretched green on either side. Somewhere ahead, a future was waiting — messy and beautiful and full of possibility.*

*Anne pressed the accelerator and drove into it.*

*HOME.*
